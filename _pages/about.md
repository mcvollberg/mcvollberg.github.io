I am a cognitive scientist studying social decision making. I lead an independent research group at the Zurich Center for Neuroeconomics (University of Zurich), supported by an SNSF Ambizione grant, in collaboration with the Swiss Center for Affective Sciences (University of Geneva). I completed my PhD in Cognition, Brain, & Behavior at Harvard University (with Mina Cikara, Daniel Schacter, and Elizabeth Phelps), followed by a SNSF postdoctoral fellowship. 

My work draws on behavioral experiments, neurophysiological measurement, and computational approaches at the intersection of affective science, behavioral economics, cognitive science, and social psychology. It has appeared in journals including Cognition, Emotion, Nature Human Behaviour, Trends in Cognitive Sciences, Current Directions in Psychological Science, PNAS, and Journal of Experimental Psychology: General. I have been recognized with the Young Scientist Award of the Swiss Psychological Society and multiple teaching distinctions from Harvard.
